Search Value Investors Club (VIC) for deep fundamental write-ups on: $ARGUMENTS

VIC is a members-only community of professional value investors. Submitted ideas are vetted and write-ups are unusually deep — typically 2,000–10,000 words covering business model, financials, valuation, catalysts, and risks. Public can read with a 45-day delay; members read in real time.

**Search method:**
VIC's own search is members-only, so use Google: `site:valueinvestorsclub.com $ARGUMENTS`. Run 2 angled queries:
- `site:valueinvestorsclub.com $ARGUMENTS` — general
- `site:valueinvestorsclub.com $ARGUMENTS long` or `short` — to filter by direction

**WebFetch the top 3–5 write-ups** and extract:
- The thesis in one sentence (every VIC piece states this up front)
- Business description: what the company actually does, segments, competitive position
- The variant view: *why* the author thinks the market is wrong (this is the entire point of a VIC piece — if it's not stated, the idea is weak)
- Valuation method and target (DCF, sum-of-parts, earnings-power-value, multiple-based)
- Stated catalysts and their timing
- Risks section (VIC requires authors to list these honestly)
- Author rating from members (scored 1–10) and the comment-thread debate
- Date posted — older write-ups may have been validated/invalidated by what happened next

**Track-record check:**
For each cited author, look at their other VIC submissions to gauge hit rate. A 7+ rated idea from a member with multiple prior wins is meaningfully different from a 4-rated debut.

**Fallback:** If VIC's public delay hides the idea, search for the author's name + ticker on Twitter/X or substack — many VIC contributors also publish on those platforms in less restricted form.

**Do NOT** rely on VIC alone — the community has a long-bias and a value-trap risk. Cross-reference with /sec-search for primary filings and /macrotrends-search for whether the "cheap" multiple is structurally cheap or temporarily cheap. Skip write-ups with member ratings under 5.

Return: thesis summary, variant view, valuation method and target, catalysts, risks, member rating, and the VIC URL.
