Search Seeking Alpha for investor theses, earnings reactions, and dividend analysis on: $ARGUMENTS

Seeking Alpha is the largest open marketplace of long/short equity write-ups. Its value is in **structured bull/bear arguments** — not the headline calls. Most articles are paywalled but the abstract, key takeaways, and comments are usually public.

**Article search:**
Use WebSearch with `site:seekingalpha.com $ARGUMENTS` and run at least 2 angled queries:
- `site:seekingalpha.com $ARGUMENTS bull case` — find long theses
- `site:seekingalpha.com $ARGUMENTS short OR risk OR overvalued` — find bearish counter-theses
- `site:seekingalpha.com $ARGUMENTS earnings` — find post-earnings reaction analysis
- `site:seekingalpha.com $ARGUMENTS dividend` — for income-focused coverage

**WebFetch the top 4–6 articles** to extract:
- The author's stated rating (Buy/Hold/Sell/Strong Buy/Strong Sell) and price target if any
- The 3–5 bullet "Key Takeaways" section (Seeking Alpha shows these even on paywalled pieces)
- Author credibility signals: contributor's follower count, performance track record, prior calls on the same name
- Comment-thread debate (often visible without subscription) — minority dissents in comments are frequently more useful than the article itself

**Earnings reaction angle:**
After earnings, search `$ARGUMENTS Q[X] earnings site:seekingalpha.com` to find the spectrum of bull/bear interpretations of the same print — divergence here is the actual signal.

**Fallback:** If the article is hard-paywalled, fetch the Google cache version or read the headline + first paragraph + comment section. Skip articles by contributors with no follower count or track record visible.

**Do NOT** treat any single contributor as an oracle — Seeking Alpha is a marketplace, not vetted research. Cross-reference rated calls with /sec-search or /macrotrends-search before relying on them. Skip "Premium" subscriber-only pieces with no public summary.

Return: bull thesis, bear thesis, contested points, contributor credibility notes, and source URLs.
