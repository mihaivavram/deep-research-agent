Search Substack for independent analyst newsletters on: $ARGUMENTS

Substack hosts the long tail of independent macro, quant, sector, and single-stock analysts who left sell-side / buy-side to publish directly. The value is **idiosyncratic deep work** that doesn't appear in mainstream financial media — but quality varies wildly, so author vetting matters.

**Search method:**
Use WebSearch with `site:substack.com $ARGUMENTS` and run at least 2 angled queries:
- `site:substack.com $ARGUMENTS` — general topic
- `site:substack.com $ARGUMENTS analysis OR thesis` — to surface deep pieces over headline posts
- `site:substack.com $ARGUMENTS macro` or `quant` or `sector` for specific lenses

**WebFetch the top 4–6 posts** and extract:
- The author's stated background (most Substack writers have an "About" page — check for ex-buy-side, ex-sell-side, academic, or hobbyist status)
- Subscriber count (visible on most publication pages — a publication with 10k+ paid subs has been market-vetted)
- The post's central claim and supporting evidence
- Whether the post is paywalled or free; if paywalled, the public preview usually contains the thesis
- Publication date — Substack newsletters can age fast, especially macro

**Authoritative finance Substacks to prioritize when relevant:**
- Doomberg, Net Interest, The Diff, Stratechery (tech-finance), Concoda (Fed plumbing), Apricitas Economics (data-driven macro), The Last Bear Standing, Bear Cave (short ideas), Hedgeye, Above Avalon (single-company deep dives)

**Fallback:** If a specific Substack post is hard-paywalled, search the author's name on Twitter/X — most Substack authors cross-post threads of the same content for free.

**Do NOT** treat Substack as authoritative on its own — it is opinion journalism. Always cross-reference factual claims with /sec-search, /fred-search, or /macrotrends-search depending on the topic. Skip Substacks with fewer than ~1k subscribers and no notable author background.

Return: key claims, author credibility, subscriber-count signal, publication date, and source Substack URLs.
