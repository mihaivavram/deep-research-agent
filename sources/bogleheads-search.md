Search the Bogleheads forum for long-term passive investing and ETF/fund analysis on: $ARGUMENTS

Bogleheads is the gold standard community for long-term, low-cost, index-fund-driven investing. Its value is **decades of accumulated wisdom on portfolio construction, tax efficiency, and behavioral pitfalls** — not stock picks. Use it for any question about asset allocation, ETF/fund comparison, retirement accounts, or tax-loss harvesting.

**Forum search:**
Use WebSearch with `site:bogleheads.org $ARGUMENTS` and run 2–3 angled queries:
- `site:bogleheads.org $ARGUMENTS` — general
- `site:bogleheads.org $ARGUMENTS vs` — for fund/ETF head-to-head debates (e.g. "VTI vs VTSAX")
- `site:bogleheads.org $ARGUMENTS allocation` — for portfolio-construction discussions

**WebFetch the top 4–6 forum threads** and extract:
- The original poster's question and constraints (age, account types, tax bracket, time horizon)
- The accepted answer or highest-upvoted reply
- The classic "Three-Fund Portfolio" or "Lazy Portfolio" references when relevant
- Disagreements among long-time members (often a thread will have two camps — both are usually defensible)
- Tax-efficiency considerations: which fund in which account type (taxable vs. IRA vs. 401k vs. HSA)
- Expense ratio and tracking-error data quoted in replies

**Wiki layer:**
Bogleheads also maintains a wiki at `https://www.bogleheads.org/wiki/`. Pages like "Tax-efficient fund placement", "Three-fund portfolio", and "Asset allocation" are canonical and worth fetching directly when relevant.

**Fallback:** If a forum thread is locked or hard to load, the Bogleheads wiki usually covers the same topic in a more authoritative form. Use the wiki as the canonical source and the forum as the messy-but-useful debate layer.

**Do NOT** use Bogleheads for active stock picking or market timing — the entire community will (correctly) tell you not to. Skip threads with fewer than ~5 replies (not enough vetting).

Return: consensus recommendations, disagreements where they exist, tax/account placement guidance, and source URLs (forum threads and wiki pages).
