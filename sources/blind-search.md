Search Blind (teamblind.com) for verified-employee anonymous workplace discussion on: $ARGUMENTS

Blind is uniquely valuable: every poster is verified as an employee of a specific company via work email, but posts anonymously. This produces **the highest-signal source on the open web for**: real comp data, layoff sentiment, internal politics, RTO policies, manager quality, performance review processes, and "what's it actually like inside Company X" questions. No other source matches Blind for this.

**Primary search strategy:**
Use Google site-search:
- `site:teamblind.com $ARGUMENTS` — general
- `site:teamblind.com "$ARGUMENTS" salary` — comp-specific
- `site:teamblind.com "$ARGUMENTS" layoff` or `pip` or `RTO` or `culture` — angled queries
- For specific companies: `site:teamblind.com "Company Name" $ARGUMENTS`

**WebFetch the top 4–6 Blind threads** and extract:
- The original post (verified by company tag — e.g. "Google", "Meta", "Amazon")
- Up/down votes on the original post (high net-positive = community-validated)
- Top comments — often more useful than the OP, especially when other companies' employees chime in with comparisons
- Any specific data: TC (total comp) numbers, level designations, signing bonuses, severance packages, RTO mandates, attrition rates
- Counter-replies that contradict the OP — Blind has strong norm of calling out exaggeration

**What makes Blind uniquely valuable:**
- **Verified employees**: posts tagged with the verified company prevent the "anyone can claim anything" problem of Reddit/Glassdoor
- **Comp transparency**: most credible source for real total-comp packages at FAANG / unicorn / startup tiers, including unvested RSU, refreshers, and bonus structures
- **Layoff signal**: employees often post layoff news on Blind hours before press coverage
- **Cross-company comparisons**: threads about "Is Meta or Google better for L6 ICs?" produce structured side-by-sides you can't find anywhere else
- **Honest culture takes**: complaints land harder here than on Glassdoor because anonymity removes retaliation fear

**Fallback strategies:**
1. If a Blind thread is gated to logged-in users, **Google's cached snippet usually contains the key claim** — paraphrase from snippet and link the original
2. For comp data specifically, cross-reference with `levels.fyi` (if asked, write a `levels-search.md` skill) and `/glassdoor-search`
3. For layoff topics, cross-reference with `layoffs.fyi` and `/news-search`

**Do NOT** trust unverified posts (Blind sometimes shows posts with missing company tags — these are anonymous trolls). Skip threads with low vote counts (under ~10 net upvotes). Don't quote individual numbers as gospel — comp ranges and the *median* claim across multiple posts is the signal, not any single data point.

Return: company-specific findings, comp data (with caveats and ranges, not single points), culture/management/RTO signals, layoff intel with dates, and source teamblind.com URLs. Always note the verified company tag of each cited post.
